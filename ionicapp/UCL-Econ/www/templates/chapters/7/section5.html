Why does the price go that way when there is excess supply? Here are three possible reasons:

  1. Some sellers notice that they have a lot of their stuff piling up. They decide that, if they offer to sell for a little less than the going price, maybe they can get some sales away from their competitors.
    This might be a reason why the price would fall in a free market with excess supply.
    This does not sound like a reason for the price to fall in a free market with excess supply.

  2. Buyers notice that the sellers' stuff is piling up. These buyers start looking for sellers willing to sell at a little less than the going price.
    This might be a reason why the price would fall in a free market with excess supply.
    This does not sound like a reason for the price to fall in a free market with excess supply.

  3. The government sees that there is a surplus and orders that the price be lowered.
    This might be a reason why the price would fall in a free market with excess supply.
    This does not sound like a reason for the price to fall in a free market with excess supply.
